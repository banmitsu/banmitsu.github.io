Dynamic Imagery

  • Anonymous
  • 2015/09/30
  • Comments
  • clouds, dynamic imagery

Dynamic imagery of landscape photographs enables an engaging viewing experience and has long been an active area of multimedia research. The creation of motions in images creates an illusion of movement, thereby making a vivid and more interesting sense of visual perception.


slides<<<<

Motivation

Photograph of a scene encapsulates a single moment in time. By adding motion content in a still photo, we can create an illusion of expanding the moment to make the viewers better engage in the viewing experience. In other words, motion enhancement of the content makes a photo more than a picture.

Recently, the related technologies have been further applied to certain commercial products such as the interactive newspaper from Harry Potter movies and the Auto Awesome app of Google.

Another similar example of such media is Cinemagraphs. These cinemagraphs are derived from a short video by retaining motion content in selected scene components, e.g., a dynamic wisp of smoke in a time stop scene.

Microsoft Research generalizes from cienemgraphs and uses a different term, Cliplets, to denote media in which the dynamic elements do not need to be strictly looping.

In this work, we explore the creation of a novel category of media, Dynamic Imagery, from still photographs, which can be more dynamic in essence than images and contains temporal elements, but does not need to be as fully dynamic as like normal videos (i.e., specific regions are kept static in a scene).

More importantly, the ways of making cinemagraphs or cliplets are both different from the proposed visual medium that is enriched with created motions from a single image, instead of using a set of photographs or a video.
